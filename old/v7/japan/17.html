Osaka Asahi Brewery

Posted in Japan, Travel | June 7, 2011 Comments Off

Before going to Kyoto today, my brother and I planned on visiting the Asahi Brewery in Osaka. Asashi Super Dry is one of the "big 3" beers of Japan, alongside Kirin and Sapporo beers. There are other ubiquitous Japanese beers, but these are the top 3.

Several other groups arrived at the brewery with us, but we were the only English speakers. They found a tour guide who spoke English and we ended up getting a personalized tour. The brewery had a whole wall of classic Asahi cans showing the commercialized history of the beer. We got to see the factory lines and canning process. At the end of the tour, we got to sample some beers, too.

Tsutenkaku Tower 通天閣

Posted in Japan, Travel | June 6, 2011 Comments Off

In Den Den Town, at the end of the street, is Tsutenkaku Tower, otherwise known as Osaka Tower. It's a very characteristic landmark of Osaka with its brilliant neons lights seen from anywhere in town after you get up past the 5th floor on a building.

The current tower is the second version. The original tower was built in 1912 to mimic the Eiffel Tower in Paris. It was destroyed in World War II by fire bombings. After the war, citizens lobbied to have the tower rebuilt and the Tsutenkaku Kanko Company was established to rebuild the tower and encourage tourism. It opened in 1956, and their efforts gave Tokyo the inspiration to begin building Tokyo Tower the next year.

The tower is best viewed at night, which is when my brother and I visited it after a long day of many other tourist attractions. Below are some photos.

Osaka Castle 大阪城

Posted in Japan, Travel | June 6, 2011 Comments Off

Osaka Castle was the center of unifying Japan ending the warring states period. Now it is one of Japan's most famous landmarks.

I am a big fan of Japanese castles, and my brother and I visited many on our trip to Osaka in 2011. Throughout our trip, and seeing more and more castles, these days, there are 3 different types of castles in Japan. There are castles which keep the original woodwork and layout since their construction. There are castles which have been completely gutted and had their insides completely replaced with a modern building turned into a full museum. And there is a combination of the two, where the interior hasn't been completely replaced, but it's been updated and some things moved around for ease of movement.

Throughout Japan, there are very few castle remaining in their original state. And I think the most common state of castles these days are the ones whose interior haven't been completely replaced, but changed slightly. Osaka Castle is one whose interior had been completely replaced with a modern building. I had these kind of castles the most because it removes almost all sense of history and personality of the castle. All that remains is the pretty facade on the outside.

The Momofuku Ando Instant Ramen Museum

Posted in Japan, Travel | June 6, 2011 Comments Off

When he was 48 years old, Momofuku Ando created the first package of precooked instant noodles in 1958 during Japan's food crisis post-war. From there, he established his company, Nissin and sold his noodles across Japan. It was a bit expensive at first, but over time the price dropped allowing more people access to the noodles. In 1971, he invented cup noodles, which allowed people to have a complete meal with only a little bit of hot water. No longer, did they need a stove or bowl to cook the noodles.

The Momofuku Ando Instant Ramen Noodle museum pays tribute to the man who invented one of Japan's most renowned products. You can see a replica of his wooden shack where he invented the noodles, travel through the history and evolution of the brand, and even make your own cup noodles!

The Osamu Tezuka Manga Museum

Posted in Japan, Travel | June 6, 2011 Comments Off

In Osaka, there is a museum dedicated to Osamu Tezuka, a legendary manga and anime author. He is considered by many as the Father or Godfather of Manga. Throughout his 60 years of life, he wrote and drew tons of manga, including Astro Boy, Kimba the White Lion, Princess Knight, Black Jack, and many more. He cartoonist style of drawing inspired generations of manga artists which gave rise to the overall anime style of drawing in Japan.
