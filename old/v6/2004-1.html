Chili Ramen on a Chilly Day

Today, I finally went through my archive pages and updated them. Also, I played a lot of Viewtiful Joe. I'm really surprised how much I like it despite being a straight-up action game. Even though it is difficult, it's not impossible, and it feels so satisfying to make progress.

Anyway, earlier today I had not eaten much, so I went through my bag of ramen and choose Maruchan's Ramen Noodle Soup Chili Flavor.

To recall, Maruchan is an American brand of instant ramen that you can buy at nearly any grocery outlet. The package says, "Careful, hot hot hot" HA! Compared to the depths of hell where the Asian countries mined their spices, this doesn't event come CLOSE. Ahahhahah. You have no idea the meaning of the word 'hot' Try that Nong Shin Hot & Spicy, and your eyes will BLEED. This is mild at best.

Besides that, this stuff was actually pretty good. It was much better than the Roast Chicken flavored stuff from before. Strong noodles. Bland seasoning, though. I give it the same score as the other Maruchan ramen, whatever it was. Probably 3 out of 5 stars.

Back to Viewtiful Joe and more drinking!

January 30, 2004

Hot Ramen on a Cold Day

Sigh... My car will not work. It has been frozen solid. Today was even colder than yesterday. When I left this morning for school, it was cold. Cold Cold Cold. It was so cold that some girl was apparently suffereing from pnuemonia, shivering to death as the paramedics were revitalizing her. Rather horrific.

Anyway, I revitalized myself with more noodles. To start the night, I choose Mama Brand Instant Noodle Pa-Lo Duck Flavored.

I remembered the last time I had Duck ramen. It was extrememly spicey. This time was not. Again, I added an egg, so I think that soaked up most of the spiciness and made for a smoother taste. I give Mama Brand Pa-Lo Duck Flavor a 3 1/2 out of 5 stars.

Next on the menu was Wai Wai Brand Tom Yum Shrimp Clear Soupl Flavour Instant Noodle.

My theory of eggs soaking up the spice was completely thrown out the window here. *cough* ack.. I thought my cooking ability had gotten around this death level of spice, but I was wrong. The big problem was that all of the spices were in a single pouch. They weren't broken up into separate packets like a GOOD ramen company would do. 1 1/2 out of 5 stars.

Man, I almost completely drank an entire jug of V8 Splash eating this ramen. And it looked so good, too! What happened? Ugh, my stomach is going to be pissed off all night long. And I'm hot. Too hot. And I'm out of cereal. NooOoooooOOOOOOoooo!

--

Meanwhile, later, I went to the grocery store, and bought some American ramen, Maruchan's Ramen Noodle Soup Roast Chicken Flavor. This is Seb's ramen of choice.

The noodles were very strong compared to the ramen from the Asian market. But, the soup was very bland. It tasted like flavored water. And adding an egg while cooking didn't help out anything. Overall, it wasn't terrible, so Maruchan's Roast Chicken Ramen gets a 3 out of 5 stars.

I bought another Maruchan ramen too. I'll probably eat it tomorrow.

January 28, 2004

Mama and Waiwai

I was quite hungry after class this afternoon, so I made some ramen. ^_^

First off, after I ran to the store to pick up some eggs, I made Mama Brand Oriental Style Instant Noodle Artificial Shrimp Flavour (Tom Yum). This leads me to believe that Tom Yum = Artificial Shrimp. Or, maybe Tom Yum = Chili Shrimp. I'm not quite sure. Inside the package was a seperate pouch of chili powder. Thank god! I was able to set the spicy level to my liking. I only used a pinch.

After cooking the ramen in my usual way, it turned out quite good. I mixed in the egg a lot so there weren't any huge chunks left in the bowl, only a few bits here and there. The spiciness level was perfect, which made this dish very enjoyable. I give Mama Brand Shrimp (Tom Yum) Noodles a 4 out of 5 stars. It gets a high rating for having separate chili powder, and tasting very very good. The noodles were strong, and cooked well. The soup had an excellent taste. And, with the egg inside bring the total to 4 1/2. The whole dish actually looked pleasing too. ^_^

One batch of ramen never really does it for me. I made a second batch of Wai Wai Brand Instant Noodles Oriental Style.

The package kinda looked like it was advertising watermelon candy, though. I used the usually approach to cooking: boiled water, added noodles, seasoning, then egg, cooked again for 2 minutes, and covered for a minute or so. LET ME TELL YOU!, the noodles were fan-fucking-tastic. It was like they were slightly cooked and seasoned beforehand. Very very tasty! And, like the previous ramen, chili powder was packaged seperately from the main powder.

How did it taste? Excellent! Oh my, very very good.

There is a problem though, the seasoning for the soup had no real context, no flavor. I could taste the chili, but nothing from the other two packs of seasoning. It was disapointing for such good noodles. I must give this ramen, as a whole, 4 out of a 5 stars because of the flavor failings. It is sad, but I've still got plenty more ramen left to go. If Wai Wai can pull together its tasty noodles with tasty flavor, then this will probably be my perferred ramen. But I have not yet found it.

Now to face the cold and go to my last class of the day, and then work. I think tonight will be a fried-rice night. I don't think my stomach likes all of these watery meals. Eating ramen is basically drinking a bowl of water. You won't gain any weight from it, but it doesn't really sound all that healthy either. Anywho, if anyone wants some fried-rice, lemme know when i get home tonight. I will put enough rice in the cooker. ^_^

January 27, 2004

Advertising and Ramen

Yesterday, for the first time as SPAMM publicity officer, I actually went around campus and put up flyers about weekly meetings. I've made posters before, but this was the first year of actually doing the advertising.

Here are the SPAMM 2004 Ads I put up around campus: Full Metal Panic, X TV, Great Teacher Onizuka, Lodoss War, Stellvia, and Berserk. I'm in love with the Lodoss War poster. ^_^

After SPAMM, I ate another batch of ramen with my last egg. This was one of my few remanining pro ramens, Mama's Oriental Style Instant Chand Noodles Beef Flavor.

Beef isn't a very common ramen flavor, for some reason. It's usually some kind of "oriental" this or "oriental" that. I wasn't afraid of it being spicy. None of the previous Chand noodle dishes had strong spice at all.

To cook the noodles, first, I boiled the water. When the water came to a boil, I added in the noodles. After they broke apart and started to become soft, I added the seasoning. When adding the egg, this time, I made sure to mix it thoroughly into the contents of the bowl. Seconds after putting the egg into the very hot water, it almost instantly began to cook. Wicked. I continued stirring and cooking for about 30 seconds, and then covered it for a while.

In the end, it tasted pretty good! I think the egg really helps out A LOT! I'm adding an egg to the ramen every single time from now on. I think it adds a lot to the dish. I'm also getting better at cooking ramen too, so that is improving the taste, too. There is definitely a science to cooking instant noodles. Most America noodles have no finesse and no science to the cooking method. These Asian imports take a little more effort, but the results are worth it.

In this case, too, I think I really prefer the beef flavor compared with others. In the end, I'm giving Mama's Beef Flavored Chand Noodles a 2 1/2 out of 5 stars. But, with the egg added into the mix, I think it elevates it to the 3 1/2.

So, today I have learned that eggs equal better ramen. I suggest you try it out next time you make ramen. You won't be disappointed.

January 26, 2004

Triple Threat

Tonight, I made three Instant noodle meals out of my pile.

---

I started off quite terribly with Wai Wai's Minced Pork Tom Yum Flavour Instant Noodle

First off, the noodle were very dark and crisp and did not cook very well. Even though I cooked them an extra minute and a half, they were still crispy. I poured more boiling water on them (and all over my counter), and covered them for an additional two minutes. They still tasted pretty stale.

But, I gotta give them credit. I did finish eating the whole thing despite their spiciness. Maybe I'm getting used to the spice, or maybe I was just really hungrey. None the less, I give Wai Wai's Tom Yum Flavored Noodles 1 star out of a possible 5. These noodles really sucked.

On the positive end of things, I tried another Kung Fu ramen, Ve Wong's Kung Fu Instant Oriental Noodle Soup, Chicken Flavor.

Much like their Shrimp dish, it is very very smooth. And the Chicken Flavor adds an American taste to it. Though, unlike the previous Kung Fu ramen, I cooked it for less time to allow the noodles to keep some strength. And it came out really well. I should have experimented with some eggs, but oh well.

I gave Kung Fu Chicken Ramen 3 out of 5 stars. I liked it, but it's still not all that professionally made, in my opinion. The Shin Ramyun noodles are way better.

I'm still somewhat hungry, so I'm going to make a third for tonight. I choose Wai Wai's Tom Klong Flavour Instant Noodle quick!

This wasn't my ideal choice after the failure of the earlier Wai Wai crap. But, this was packaged differently, as you'll notice in the picture above. It is easily recognized by the "Oh my!" face on the front.

I wanted to experiment a little bit this time. After I boiled the water, I put in the noodles, seasoning, and also an egg on top. I mixed it up, and then cooked it for 2 minutes. Not a smart idea as the egg was cooked too much in those 2 minutes. In the end, the bowl had large chunks of egg in it. I probably should have cooked it for 1 minute instead. it didn't bother me, though, its just egg and ramen. But, like I had hoped, the egg did soak up a lot of the flavor in the soup, which made eating the spicy stuff more bearable.

It was actually pretty good. I give Wai Wai Quick! Tom Klong Flavor, otherwise known as the noodles with the face on it, a 3 1/2 out of 5 stars. I was quite an upgrade, considering the other crap earlier. Although, I think the egg helped this one A LOT. The noodles themselves tasted very good without being cooked, too. Tasty crunchy noodles.

Bowling class kicks ass. It's the perfect stress reliever during a semester full of projects and homework. It brings back memories of being on my high school bowling team for 5 years.

January 21, 2004

Ichiban Shrimp

Today's instant dinner is Sapporo Ichiban's Japanese Style Noodles & Shrimp Flavored-soup.

To cook this one, you boil water, then put the noodles into the boiling water and then continue to boil for 3 minutes. After which, you add in the seasoning. The noodles were quite delicious. Boiling the noodles really brought out their texture. The seasoning was pretty weak though, especially compared to the deathly spicy stuff on the previous noodle dishes. But since it wasn't strong, I was actually able to eat the whole thing for a change!

I give Sapporo Ichiban's Japanese Style Noodles & Shrimp Flavored soup a high rating: 4 out of 5 stars. It was good, but something tells me that there is something better out there. I've still got plenty more ramen to try out.

January 19, 2004

Myojo and Mama

Keeping up with journal entries, I ate 2 more Asian market instant noodle dishes last night.

First off is Myojo's Instant Noodle Duck Flavour.

Like the previous duck-flavored instant meal I've eaten, I first tested the spiciness of the spice packets included. True enough, the packet was extremely spicy. But there was only one pack, so i just dumped everything in there and didn't really care all that much.

This instant meal was one of those where you pour boiling water into a bowl of noodles and seasoning. Then, let the whole thing cook by itself for a couple minutes after that.

After it was done, the whole thing had a very strong smell, almost too strong. *cough* The noodles had a very plastic texture, too. They didn't taste like noodles at all and felt very stale. I may have gotten some fucked up package of this stuff, but overall, I think it was not very good.

I give Myojo's Instant Noodle Duck Flavour 1 out of 5 stars. blahck.. akc.. Not delicious.

Next up is Mama's Instant Noodles Poh Tak Flavour.

I pre-tasted for spiciness, and it was actually rather smooth for a change. It was an interesting change compared to the others.

These noodles were prepared in the same way to the normal instant noodles. Poor in boiling water, then cover for 3 minutes. This method did not work out at all, though. The noodles were still very stiff after the 3 minute mark. I think from now on i'm going to cook all these meals for 3 minutes after I poor in the boiling water.

The poh tak flavor ended up being too spicy to consume after all, which was sad. I thought I had finally found a good tasting ramen. Unfortunately, Mama's Instant Noodles Poh Tak flavour receives 2 out of 5 stars. Not all that great.

January 6, 2004

Vermicelli Duck

Today's meal, just like most meals, was boguht from the Asian Market in Brooklyn Center. Though, unlike most days where I would make rice, and then mess around with it, today I made one of those Instant meals they had available for a dime. Today's experimentation of flavor comes from Thailand in the form of Mama's Instant Rice Vermicelli Duck Flavour .

In my professional opinion: this stuff is weird. Normally, you'd think a food item with the word duck in it would taste normal. But, damm...

First off, the rice vermicelli noodles looked more like a chunk of shredded wheat than actual noodles. Secondly, after I put the seasoning on the noodles, they smell was weird and quite intoxicating. It was definitely not normal. That should have tipped me off.. the seasoning came in 3 different pouches that I could not read what-so-ever, so i dumped all three onto the chunk and then boiled some water to begin.

After the cooking process was complete, it was time to eat. The noodles were pretty delicious, not the best, but it was good. After the noodles were eaten, I started in on the liquid. *cough cough* .. *ack.. hack !!*.. ack.. Holy spicy..*cough cough*. Man, this stuff is extremely spicy. I couldn't even finish it. I think that third seasoning pouch was extra spicy seasoning meant for those people that drink tabasco sauce daily. I bought two of these instant duck things, so I think I'll try again minus to tiny pouch of death spice.

Otherwise not bad! I give Mama's Instant Rice Vermicelli Duck Flavour 2 out of 5 stars. Any instant meal that I cannot finish loses alot of points in my book, even if it did cost a dime.
