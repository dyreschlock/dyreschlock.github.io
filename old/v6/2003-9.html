September 21, 2003

The Inferno

After buying some necessasities, I journeyed to Club Inferno FINALLY to see what this club is all about after hearing about it last year. Inferno is located on the far east side of Madison, way the hell away from any distinguishing landmarks that would make it easy to find at night. After driving past the correct roads several times, I finally got there around 10pm.

Interestingly enough, taking a wrong turn through some community center brought me upon a RAVE. I was going around the parking lot when I noticed a bunch of crazy lights coming from the windows off to my right. I parked the car and walked closer to confirm my initial thoughts. It was a pretty high class rave, too. Everyone was in prom suitable attire. The layout of the interior looked very similiar to Chain Reaction II/IV, back in the day. My ears caught drift of some very poor house sounding music, so i darted back to my car to continue on to Inferno.

Club Inferno is damn schweet. Wow... It's much different than both The Quest and Visions. The Quest and Visions are actual night clubs where Inferno is a goth+fetish club. Also, Inferno is in an urban setting, so parking is free.

I finally got to hear the sounds of DJ Whiterabbit on his home turf and wow. He plays some very powerful music. Very german sounding hard house, or perhaps gothic house? It's an excellent sound. Maybe I should get into it. ^_^ Besides Whiterabbit, DJ Eurotic was tag teaming the action, and had no concept of beat matching. It was extremely annoying. On the plus side, though, his record selection was not terrible.

The night proved to be most enjoyable, especially watching the goth/fetish people go crazy on the floor. Man, they really get into the whole morbid style of dance. A few guys danced as if they were restrained in a straight jacket. Wicked. Were they dancing, or trying to break free? This other guy really had the look. He had a neck and wrist collars, and then just cutoff jean shorts with some pretty dark looking boots. He danced like he was in a fight to the death. Everyone danced very energic and mostly dressed in black, probably because they couldn't find anything darker to match their souls.

I took around 70 some pictures, but they were all taken sitting in the same spot, so I really only have about 2-4 pictures that should be put up. And now for sleep. My eyes are tired.

September 14, 2003

Delerium (Live) @ The Quest, Minneapolis

These photos are from Delerium's live performance at The Quest (now Epic) in Minneapolis, MN on September 14th, 2003. Just one day after DJ Micro, I stayed in Minneapolis for this event. I loved Tiesto's "In Search of Sunrise" remix of their track, Silence. I was also in love with Satoshi Tomiie's remix of "After All"

I figured I love to hear them live. But, I did not prepare myself very well for the event. Like all the events I had been to prior, I figured it would smooth tunes, and dancing all night long, but it was simply a concert. And I wasn't very well familiar with their original tracks.

So, I didn't have that great of a time, but that's fine.

Note: This post was made on March 22nd, 2014 and back-dated to the time of the event.

September 13, 2003

DJ Micro @ The Quest, Minneapolis

These photos are from DJ Micro's performance at The Quest (now Epic) in Minneapolis, MN on September 13th, 2003.

I remember being disappointed by this event. I was huge fan of Micro's Tech Mix CDs he had at the time. I must've listened to version 2.0 a hundred times. I loved that CD. But his live performance didn't really do it for me. I didn't hear the style he represented on his CDs. Those CDs were 3 or 4 years out of date at this point, and I think Micro's style started going in a different direction.

Armin @ Visions, Spundae, Chicago

After looking at some of the responses of Armin van Buuren and Seneca at Visions, Spundae last Saturday, I do regret not going. One reason being that I've never been to Spundae when there was a DJ that I was actually REALLY into, music genre wise. The first time I was there was for Nigel Richards. He played some nice stuff, but I'm not that big a fan of house. And then the second/most recent time was Christopher Lawrence. American trance, one or two tracks were really badass, but other than that it was rather stale.
