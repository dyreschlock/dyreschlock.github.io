Dance Dance Revolution Max Preview

The BeMani series debuts on the Playstation 2, May 16th, 2002. DDRmax features some new features not present in previous DDRs. These features include Duration notes, a revision of the difficulty method, and the return of random song selection.

The Duration notes are notes held for an amount of time. You'd recognize these from Para Para Paradise.

The revision of the difficulty system does away with the number of steps and instead show a graph.

Before you start the songs, you select your difficulty. Light, Standard, and Heavy, replace Basic, Trick, and Maniac. (I think that's the right order. I always get Trick and Maniac mixed up.)

The difficulty graph replaces the steps with 5 categories. Most are self-explanatory. Steam (overall density of the song), Chaos (degree of irregularity), Freeze (degree of duration notes), Air (degree of jump), and Voltage (maximum density).

For you professional DDRers, here's a list of the songs and arrows for you to practice memorizing ^_^: http://www.hx.sakura.ne.jp/~zyx-19/ddrmax.html
