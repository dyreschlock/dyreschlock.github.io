Adobe Max Day One - Effective Website Redesign

Strategy

Another bit of strategy is Data or Content.Â  Now that you have your goals, what content do you really need and what can you ditch.Â  Do you really need a detailed account of Adobe's History on the front page of Adobe.com?Â  Do you really need it on the site at all?Â  If so, where should it go so it's not in the way of pushing your product line?

Redesign v. Reskin

When redesigning your site.. do you really need to completely overall the entire thing?Â  Maybe a fresh coat of paint will accomplish your goals.

Skinning is an easy way to design what you already have in a different way.Â  If the company changes focus from a family friendly environment to something with a more business oriented approach, switching out the graphics and redoing the CSS would probably be the best result, if they didn't want the content to change at all.

Another thing you may not think about is Priority.Â  What do you want the User to see on your site?Â  What isn't so important on your site that you shouldn't spend too much time on?

Remember that the overall goal of a redesign is to make your site as good or better than the previous version, so if something simply isn't working, maybe you should rethink your decision and revisit your previous version.

Design

Once you've developed your goals, and begun to form an idea of your redesign.. Design it!Â  Start drawing mock-ups and coding prototypes.Â  As you start building your website keep a few things in mind...

Build with a redesign in mind
You're already begun to build your website, but experience tells us that everything can and will change.Â  You will be redesigning what you're working on now, so build this new design with that in mind.Â  Don't make things difficult for you in the future.

Generic Naming
Part of that is naming things generic.Â  You want to clearly label your CSS classes and ID tags so you have a general idea of what they do, and then reuse them all over your site.Â  Don't be too specific or you'll get bogged down with thousands of IDs that you just cannot maintain.

Semantic Markup
Speaking of CSS, Use it!Â  Use it alot.Â  This is rather obvious.Â  CSS is there to make designing, skinning and the web experience as editable and customizable as possible.Â  If you aren't using CSS, then you have fallen way behind of the times.

Separate Presentation from Content
Build templates, and reuse CSS and JavaScript.Â  Don't put too many details into each of your pages when you have an entire sites to build.Â  Templates are a great way of reusing code throughout the site.Â  When making a single change to your site, you don't want to make that change in a thousand places.
